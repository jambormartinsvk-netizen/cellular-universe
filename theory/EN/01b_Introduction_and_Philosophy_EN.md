## 1. The question at the beginning

When you look at the night sky, you see light that has travelled for billions of years through something we call "empty space". Yet empty space is the most mysterious thing in physics: it weighs (dark energy), it expands, it bends light, and it carries quantum correlations across the entire universe. That does not look like nothing. That looks like **something that is doing something**.

This work rests on a single question — we call it the anchor:

> **Is observed physics the surface trace of the metabolism of a cellular network of space?**

The question did not give rise to a loose metaphor but to a strict programme: every claim about the invisible must be translated into a measurable surface trace and judged against data. Whatever leaves no trace may inspire, but may not carry weight. How that turned out is told by the attached table of predictions — but first, the story.

## 2. The cast of the story (a glossary, in the order you will meet them)

**Cell.** The smallest unit of space, of Planck size (10⁻³⁵ m — as much smaller than an atom as an atom is smaller than the Solar System). Cells are not IN space; cells ARE space. Each has a **surface** — the only layer that we and all our instruments can see — and an **interior**, a separate dimension into which one cannot look, as a matter of principle. Cells do not form a tidy lattice like a chessboard but a random network like foam — and we shall see that it is precisely the randomness that saves them.

**Fuel.** The energy of the vacuum — the "nothing" which, according to quantum physics, seethes with activity. For the cells, it is food.

**Digestion.** What a cell does with fuel: it processes it at its own, internal pace — a cell has no idea how fast the universe is expanding; it only has its own clock.

**Division.** When a cell has digested enough, it splits in two. And here is the theory's first great turn: **the division of cells IS the expansion of the universe.** The universe does not expand INTO anything and nothing pushes it — it grows the way tissue grows.

**Overhead.** Division is not free. A new cell must build connections to its neighbours, and part of the fuel burns away in the process. This small fee — we denote it δ (delta) — is the heart of the whole theory: **there is no free lunch** (principle F5). Remember it; in a moment, three great mysteries will grow out of it at once. And its size is not chosen: it follows from the number of a cell's connections — on average 15.54 surface neighbours (a mathematical constant of random networks) plus 28 interior channels (the number of boson states of the Standard Model) — hence δ = 1/43.5 ≈ 0.023.

**Waste, ash, steam — the ladder of digestion.** Digestion is almost perfect, but only almost:
- **Waste** is an exceedingly rare failure (~once in 10¹²³ attempts): an undigested residue that solidifies — and that is **ordinary matter**. Every atom in the universe is such a rare error of an almost perfect process; that is why the universe is nearly empty. (And from this rare raw material everything else then grew — stars, planets, life.)
- **Ash** is waste digested to the very end: it no longer shines, reacts with nothing, it only weighs — **dark matter**. That is why half a century of detectors has never "caught" it: there is nothing to catch it by.
- **Steam** is the perfectly digested remainder: pure ripples of the network itself, which escaped in the first era and have been flying through the universe ever since. Today they form a faint background (0.9 kelvin) — and their exact amount is the sharpest prediction of the theory.

**Genesis and today.** In the first era — genesis — the cells were full of fuel and digested furiously; that is when all matter and all steam came into being. Today the fuel is diluted to 10⁻¹²³ of its original density, and the metabolism runs on only as a quiet, ember-like process.

**The accounting shadow.** The last character — and the only one that does not actually exist. A cosmologist measures the universe with a silent assumption: "the amount of matter stays the same, it merely dilutes with expansion." In our universe that does not hold — matter slowly accumulates (ash from the slow fuel channel) and the fuel thins through overhead. When an accountant with the wrong assumption balances the books of such a universe, a mysterious extra entry appears in his ledgers: something that pushes, weakens with time, and behaves slightly "supernaturally". We call that entry **dark energy**. The theory says: it is not a thing, it is a shadow in the bookkeeping — and it predicts exactly what shape it must have (and DESI measured exactly that shape in 2024).

## 3. The story: one tax, three mysteries

Now that the cast has been introduced, the theory's entire cosmology can be told in a single paragraph.

Overhead δ > 0 means the fuel slowly dilutes — it is not a perfect cosmological constant. From this follows the **first trace**: dark energy must appear to be weakening (and that is exactly how DESI sees it). The same fee means the universe held a touch more energy in its middle past than the standard model assumes — and since the total "length of the journey" to the primordial light is measured to a hundredth of a percent, it must travel more slowly today: the **second trace**, H₀ ≈ 66 (the theory thereby publicly bets against the local measurements of ~73 — either they carry a systematic error, or the theory falls). And thirdly: during genesis the fuel powered near-exponential expansion — the universe had its "inflation", but **without an inflaton**, without any new field; the metabolism did it by itself. The slow decline of the pace, set once again by the very same overhead, imprinted a slight tilt on the pattern of primordial seeds in the sky: the **third trace**, n_s = 0.9656 — a number derived, not fitted, and matching the measurement to 0.15σ.

Three great mysteries of modern cosmology — weakening dark energy, the Hubble tension, the tilt of the primordial spectrum — and beneath them a single receipt: there is no free lunch.

And the steam? Standard cosmology with an inflaton dilutes the primordial ripples to nothing. Our universe did not have inflation as an episode of a foreign field — it had it as its own fuel era — and therefore it **had to keep** its steam. Prediction: N_eff = 3.10 instead of the standard 3.045. Measurable by the end of the decade. If the steam is not there, the theory will feel it; if it is, the standard model will have nothing to explain it with.

## 4. The second network: the interior and entanglement

The interiors of cells are not isolated islands. When physics speaks of quantum entanglement — two particles attuned across half the universe — this theory says: their cells **share a piece of interior**. Alongside the surface network on which light runs, there thus exists a second, inner network of coherence (we independently converged on the ER=EPR idea from mainstream physics). Two things about it are not wishes but proven theorems: no signal may flow through the interior (or causality would fall), and no gravity may flow through it (or Newton's law would not exist — verified by a simulation which, without this condition, really does destroy Newton).

The theory's most beautiful rule belongs right here: upon division, the daughters — who a moment earlier were a single interior — **endow each other with half of their capacity**. From this single "half-dowry" rule, simulation alone yields that the inner network is always saturated at full capacity, and that its connectivity across the boundary of any region grows exactly with the AREA of that boundary (measured: exponent 1.97 ≈ 2). The area law is, in turn, precisely the ingredient from which the correct pattern of primordial seeds grows — and, independently, the famous Bekenstein principle of black holes. One rule about the sisterly love of cells, three great consequences.

## 5. The philosophical foundation (F1–F5)

**F1 — What is reality?** It arises on the surface of a cell at interaction. The interior exists independently but is not directly accessible. Reality is layered.

**F2 — Is the interior real if it cannot be observed?** Yes. There are layers of reality that are visible (electron, photon), sensed (ash — gravity only), and inaccessible. Inaccessibility is not non-existence.

**F3 — Does observation hold a privileged place?** Not because of consciousness — because of **irreversibility**. A measurement is a scar in the network that cannot be undone. A conscious observer is not special; an irreversible interaction is.

**F4 — Does the universe have a purpose?** Not as a plan. Complexity is a by-product of the tissue's growth and the accumulation of defects. But notice what that means for us: matter is waste, yet we are not merely matter. We are what grew out of the waste — billions of years of gravity, chemistry and life turned the rare errors of digestion into something that can look back and ask where it came from. The universe has no purpose; but beings have grown within it who can give themselves one. (And there is a peculiar dignity in that: we are not a plan — we are a surprise.)

**F5 — No free lunch.** The only philosophical principle that directly generates measurable numbers — and, as we have seen, it does so three times.

**Bonus — the universe's memory is provably incomplete.** Thermodynamics, the finite capacity of cells, and the overwriting of scars all lead to the same conclusion: space remembers only the strongest events. The past is not an archive. The past is a scar.
